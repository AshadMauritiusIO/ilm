The Sanctuary of Mindfulness

Finding stillness, presence, and divine connection through Remembrance (Dhikr)

Surah Al-Baqarah (2:152)
فَاذْكُرُونِي أَذْكُرْكُمْ وَاشْكُرُوا لِي وَلَا تَكْفُرُونِ
"Fadhkuroonee adhkurkum washkuroo lee wala takfuroon"
"So remember Me; I will remember you. And be grateful to Me and do not deny Me."
Mindful Reflection: A beautiful cosmic reality. The moment your heart consciously reaches out to the Divine, you are simultaneously being spoken of and remembered by the Creator of existence.
Surah Ar-Ra'd (13:28)
الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا وَتَتْمَئِنُّ قُلُوبُهُم بِذِكْرِ اللَّهِ ۗ أَلَا بِذِكْرِ اللَّهِ تَتْمَئِنُّ الْقُلُوبُ
"Allatheena amanoo watatmainnu quloobuhum bithikri Allahi ala bithikri Allahi tatmainnu alquloob"
"Those who have believed and whose hearts find rest in the remembrance of Allah. Unquestionably, by the remembrance of Allah do hearts find rest."
Mindful Reflection: True inner stillness cannot be manufactured by worldly means. The soul naturally settles into profound tranquility only when it aligns back to its Source.

Hadith Qudsi — Sahih Bukhari
يَقُولُ اللَّهُ تَعَالَى أَنَا عِنْدَ ظَنِّ عَبْدِي بِي وَأَنَا مَعَهُ إِذَا ذَكَرَنِي فَإِنْ ذَكَرَنِي فِي نَفْسِهِ ذَكَرْتُهُ فِي نَفْسِي وَإِنْ ذَكَرَنِي فِي مَلَإٍ ذَكَرْتُهُ فِي مَلَإٍ خَيْرٍ مِنْهُمْ
"Yaqoolu Allahu ta'ala: Ana 'inda zhanni 'abdee bee, wa ana ma'ahu idha dhakaranee. Fa in dhakaranee fee nafsihi, dhakartuhu fee nafsee. Wa in dhakaranee fee mala'in, dhakartuhu fee mala'in khayrin minhum..."
"Allah Almighty says: 'I am as My servant expects of Me, and I am with him when he remembers Me. If he remembers Me inwardly within himself, I will remember him within Myself. If he mentions Me in a gathering, I will mention him in a gathering far greater than them...'"
Mindful Reflection: This authentic companion piece to Sunan Ibn Majah 3792 illuminates how dynamic your relationship with Allah is. If you move toward Him walking, He meets you running. Your perception of His mercy dictates how you will experience it.

“Keep your tongue moist with the remembrance of God.”
